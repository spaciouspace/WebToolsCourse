History

I started learning Python, HTML, CSS, and Javascript through Udacity's free courses. They were so much much fun that I continued learning Python at DataCamp. Eventually, I wanted to get a more solid academic background, and feedback to fill in the gaps, and I started taking more formal community college Computer Science, Web Development, and IT courses. I like learning new things, and especially, solving complex logical puzzles. My assembly class involved making a tic tac toe game. I decided to experiment with the idea of storing multiple variables in one integer by storing all the moves for each player in one variable. Since assembly is the least readable language of all the ones I have worked with, this was a fun and harmless experiment. But, of course, I wouldn't do somehting like that in Python.
