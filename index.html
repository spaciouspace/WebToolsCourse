He was born Steven Demetre Georgiou. His girlfriend said he had the eyes of a cat. He thought his unusual name might keep him from getting famous, so he changed his name to Cat Stevens.

A few more details from Wikipedia:

Yusuf Islam (born Steven Demetre Georgiou; 21 July 1948), commonly known by his stage names Cat Stevens, Yusuf, and Yusuf / Cat Stevens, is a British singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist. His musical style consists of folk, pop, rock, and, later in his career, Islamic music. He returned to making secular music in 2006. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2014.

A biography of Cat Stevens from the perspective of an avid fan who mourned the loss of his openness and enthusiasm for life can be found in this New Yorker article.

Contrary to popular belief, he did not write the Cat's in the Cradle. People often associate the song with him because of his name, and the similar style in the same time period. But he never even remade it. It was actually written by Henry Chapin in 1974. See for yourself.
Cat's in the Cradle

He released 15 compilation albums between 1975 and 2013. I wonder if that's how he grew his fan base. One person on YouTube commented, "I'm 23, and I'm his biggest fan." I myself jumped on the train with his 1987 release, and I still find his songs inspiring. He has an impressive 387K subscribers to his personal YouTube channel. His songs, many of which are over 50 years old, have millions of views. The world is a better place because of the creativity and joy that he shared with the world through his music.
